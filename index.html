Ella Csarnó (pronounced "Char-noh") is a strategic communicator and event producer. She blends sharp strategic thinking with a creative spirit to bring complex projects to life. Ella loves to help creative people get things done, and to foster communities where people are seen and supported.

She has over a decade of experience in strategic communication, with a focus on event production and outreach. More about Ella’s professional background here.

Currently an MPS candidate in Project Management at Georgetown University with a focus on Lean and Agile, Ella holds a MA in International Communication Studies from National Chengchi University, Taiwan, where she was a Taiwan Scholarship fellow. She also holds a BA in Chinese and Italian Philology and Culture from ELTE University in Budapest. Ella studied and taught critical theory at the College for Advanced Studies in Social Theory, and was a member of the József Eötvös College, where she researched Chinese-Italian cultural ties, and taught Italian. Ella and her co-authors published her MA thesis research, In Social Media We Distrust: Investigating Users’ Hostile Media Perception of Facebook News Content.

Ella is originally from Hungary, spent a significant portion of her twenties in Taiwan, and currently lives in Washington, D.C. She’s fluent in Hungarian, English, and Italian, and also speaks German, Mandarin Chinese, and basic Hebrew.

Ella plays the drums and dances Argentine tango (but never at the same time).
